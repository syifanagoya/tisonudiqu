Early Life

Ralph Fiennes, named Ralph Nathaniel Twisleton-Wykeham-Fiennes at birth, was born on the 22nd of December, 1962 to Mark Fiennes and Jennifer Lash in Ipswich, Suffolk, England. His father was a farmer who channeled his artistic side through his love for photography, and his mother was a writer and a painter. He is the eldest child of his parents’ and has six siblings. It isn’t surprising to see that almost the entire Fiennes clan dabbles in the art of acting and writing movies.

Growing up, he moved between West Cork and County Kilkenny in Ireland. Ralph Fiennes studied at the St Kieran’s College before transferring to Newton School. After moving to England, he continued his education and settled at Bishop Wordsworth’s School. Later, he attended the Chelsea College of Art to study painting but ended up transferring to London’s Royal Academy of Dramatic Art after one year to pursue his love for acting.

Ralph Fiennes was married to Alex Kingston, but the couple divorced in 1997. After the failed marriage, Ralph dated Francesca Annis for 11 years before announcing their separation in 2006. The actor currently resides in Shoreditch.

Career

The years 1983-1985 saw Ralph Fiennes delving into his true passion, acting. He was a trainee at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art for the three years. His career commenced with work at the Open Air Theatre and the National Theatre, before garnering fame at the Royal Shakespeare Company as a Shakespeare interpreter. His film career started with him starring in “Wuthering Heights” in the year 1992.

The year 1993 saw him getting recognized as a mainstream actor with his appearance in the movie, ”The Baby of Macon.” In the same year, he received his first Academy Award Nomination for Best Supporting Actor for his role as a Nazi officer in Steven Spielberg’s “Schindler’s List.” This was only a start to his meteoric career. The year 1996 saw him receiving his first Academy Award Nomination for Best Actor for his role in the World War II centric movie.

In 2002, he starred in the film,“ Red Dragon,” which chronicles around the “Hannibal” series. His characterization of Francis Dolarhyde was widely appreciated. The year 2005 saw him playing “Lord Voldemort” in the Harry Potter film series. This role cemented him as a household name. His portrayal of the evil nemesis garnered immense appraisal. His advent into direction came with the movie rendition of the Shakespearean tragedy, “Coriolanus.”

The year 2012 saw him starring in “Skyfall,” the twenty-third film in the widely popular James Bond series. In 2014, he starred in “The Grand Budapest Hotel” in which the role of Monsieur Gustave earned him a BAFTA nomination for Best Actor and Golden Globe nomination for Best Actor.
