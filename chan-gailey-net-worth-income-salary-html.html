Thomas Chandler Gailey, Jr. (born January 5, 1952) is an American football coach who has worked at the professional and collegiate levels. Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets college football coach Chan Gailey was most recently head coach of the Buffalo Bills of the National Football League (NFL); he previously served as head coach of the NFL’s Dallas Cowboys and the Georgia Tech Yellow Jackets collegiate football team. Gailey previously worked as the offensive coordinator for the Miami Dolphins during the 2000–01 season, during which the Dolphins achieved a pair of 11–5 records consecutively. He was a member of the Pittsburgh Steelers’ coaching staff from 1994 to 1997, during which time the Steelers won four consecutive AFC Central championships and appeared in one Super Bowl (XXX). In 1997, he served as offensive coordinator for the Pittsburgh Steelers, who finished sixth in the NFL in total offence and seventh in scoring. Before being removed of his responsibilities by Kansas City Chiefs head coach Todd Haley, Gailey served as offensive coordinator for the Chiefs in 2008 and for the first three games of the 2009 preseason before being fired.
