On his educational background, he graduated with a bachelor’s degree in law from King Saud University.

Career

After Salman graduated from college, he spent many years in the private sector and later became the personal aide to his father, King Salman. Later he started working at the Saudi Cabinet, where he worked as a consultant for the Experts Commission.

He entered politics in December 2009 when he was appointed as the special adviser to his father when he was the governor of Riyadh Province. He rose from one position to another, and when the Crown Prince Sultan bin Abdulaziz died in October 2011, King Salman became the defense minister as well as the prime minister. Mohammed was appointed as his private advisor.

King Salman became the Crown king in 2012 after the death of Prince Nayef bin Abdulaziz Al Saud, and Mohammed moved to the second in hierarchy. He was appointed state minister in April 2014. His father became the king in January 2015, and Mohammed was appointed the minister of defense. He was appointed Crown Prince in June 2017.

After assuming office in 2017, Mohammed ordered a purge on everybody who was involved in corruption. This led to the arrest of many wealthy businessmen, princes, as well as government ministers. He has been very vocal on matters like human rights, domestic reforms, and other issues.  He openly criticized the United States’ decision to recognize Jerusalem as the capital of Israel.

As the Crown Prince of Saudi Arabia, Salman has been faced with several controversies, with some people even questioning his leadership. He was portrayed as a political gambler for his interventions in Yemen and Syria. He forced the Prime Minister of Lebanon, Saad Hariri, to resign in 2017.

In August 2018, he ordered the freezing of trade with Canada and also expelled Canada’s ambassador after the foreign affairs minister expressed concern over the arrest of human rights activists.
